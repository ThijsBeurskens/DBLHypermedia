Every student takes a number of basic courses. These basic courses provide the foundation for moulding the 'Eindhoven Engineer', and will develop your transversal knowledge. This is the knowledge you will need to follow electives and coherent elective packages in a degree program outside the one you have chosen. The basic courses include Mathematics, Physics, Modeling, Design, User, Society & Enterprise (USE), and Professional Skills. There are different variants of the basic courses for different degree programs.

Calculus and physics

There is no formal BC-requirement, but the content of your Calculus and Physics variant is more than relevant for a particular Major or specialization. When you change your Major or specialization during your Bachelor, it is possible you followed an other Calculus or Physics variant and you need to do self study to be able to keep up in follow-up courses. PT-Robotics: Calculus B and Physics B (cf Electrical Engineering) PT-Living: Minimum Calculus A and Physics A (cf Bouwkunde) PT-ICT: Minimum Calculus A and Physics A (cf Computer Science) Calculus B and Physics B allow for more options in electives and a possible switch to PT-Robotics.

Modeling, User, Society & Enterprise (USE) and Design

As of 2014-2015, these three basic courses are taught in one variant, so there is no possibility (and no need) to make a choice.
